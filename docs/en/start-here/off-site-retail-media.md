# What is off-site retail media?

Off-site retail media is advertising bought through a retailer's data and trust, but delivered outside its website: in the parcel it ships, in its post-purchase email, or on its social channels. The brand reaches the retailer's customers where they are already attentive, without depending on the retailer's website traffic.

## On-site or off-site: the difference

Retail media splits into two families, depending on where the ad appears.

<div class="gi-value-grid">
  <div class="gi-value-card">
    <strong>On-site retail media</strong>
    <p>Banners and sponsored products shown on the retailer's site or app during the shopping session. Limited to that site's traffic.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="gi-value-card">
    <strong>Off-site retail media</strong>
    <p>Advertising activated by the retailer's data but delivered away from its site: parcel, email, social. It follows the customer beyond the shopping session. This is the getinside model.</p>
  </div>
</div>

Off-site retail media is the fastest-growing segment, because it extends the retailer-customer relationship beyond the product page and reaches the buyer in high-attention moments.

The strength of getinside off-site retail media is **closed-loop attribution**: the QR code scan and the unique-per-distributor promo code tie each sale back to the campaign that generated it, with no third-party cookies.
