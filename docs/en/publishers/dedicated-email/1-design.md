Once notified of a new campaign, you design the email. The advertiser provides the substance (the offer), you provide the style (the tone).

## Access the brief

<div class="gi-step">
  <div class="gi-step-num">1</div>
  <div class="gi-step-body">
    <h3>Open the campaign</h3>
    <p>Log in at <a href="https://app.getinside.media/" target="_blank">app.getinside.media</a> → <strong>"Campaigns"</strong> tab → select the active campaign. You will find the commercial offer and key arguments, product visuals (logos, HD packshots), and the advertiser's redirection links.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Design the email

The goal is **native endorsement**: the message must be perceived as a sincere recommendation from you, not as an external advertisement.

<div class="gi-step">
  <div class="gi-step-num">1</div>
  <div class="gi-step-body">
    <h3>Keep your visual identity</h3>
    <p>Use your usual header, fonts, and buttons. Your subscribers recognise your newsletter — don't turn it into a billboard.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="gi-step">
  <div class="gi-step-num">2</div>
  <div class="gi-step-body">
    <h3>Write an introductory editorial</h3>
    <p>Write a personalised hook — <em>"Dear customers, here is something we wanted to share with you…"</em> — to contextualise the offer and build trust.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="gi-step">
  <div class="gi-step-num">3</div>
  <div class="gi-step-body">
    <h3>Integrate the advertiser's assets</h3>
    <p>Place the advertiser's visuals and offer at the centre of the email. Do not copy-paste HTML provided by the brand, and do not remove your logo.</p>
  </div>
</div>
