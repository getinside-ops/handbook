# Design Guide

Unboxing is an unusual moment. The consumer just received something they ordered — they're attentive, hands busy, mind open. Your flyer has less than 3 seconds to make use of it.

This guide covers everything: offer strategy, mechanics, copywriting, layout, and technical validation.

:::warning Mandatory validation
No content is distributed to a retailer without prior validation from getinside via the platform. Submit your digital proof (BAT) before any printing.
:::

## The offer

The offer is the most important decision in your flyer. A good offer compensates for a mediocre layout. The reverse is rarely true.

### Monetary value vs. percentage

:::tip "€10 off" converts better than "-10%"
Concrete value is perceived immediately. A free product works even better — the psychological pull of a gift outperforms a discount.
:::

### Validity period

:::info Minimum 3 months
Recipients often keep a flyer for several weeks before scanning it. An offer that expires too soon is a lost conversion.

Plan for at least 3 months of validity from the print date.
:::

### Promo code

A promo code reinforces the sense of exclusivity and lets you track offline conversions:

- One **code per retailer** — e.g., "GREENWEEZ20", "BERCEAU15"
- Short and memorable, no special characters (no `_`, `-`, `;`)
- Including the retailer's name or universe in the code strengthens coherence and recall

### Audience alignment

Before finalizing your mechanic, ask one question: **"Why is this content meant for this audience?"**

If the answer isn't obvious to you, it won't be obvious to the recipient either. The coherence between the retailer's universe and your offer is what separates a flyer that gets kept from one that gets discarded.

## Copywriting

### The hook

Don't open with your brand name. Open with the customer benefit.

- **Avoid:** "Discover the new [Brand] collection"
- **Prefer:** "Your beauty routine, gifted in this parcel" / "Say goodbye to [Problem] starting today"

### The promise

The offer should be the most readable element on the flyer. Use action verbs: "Get", "Receive", "Save".

### Urgency

Give a reason to act now:

- "Offer exclusive to [Partner Name] customers"
- "Valid for the next 500 orders only"

## Paper UX

### Visual hierarchy

The eye follows a Z-pattern on A5/A6 formats:

- **Top left** — Retailer logo (endorsement, reassurance)
- **Center** — The offer and product visual
- **Bottom right** — The QR code and CTA, where the thumb naturally holds a flyer

### QR code

The QR code is the only bridge to conversion.

- White background, black modules — avoid textured or light-colored backgrounds
- Leave space around the code to help the camera focus
- Always add a short instruction next to it: "Scan to claim your offer"
- **Minimum size: 25 × 25 mm** (see [technical specifications](./print-specs))

An explicit CTA ("Scan to get your €10 discount") can generate up to 10 times more scans than a vague one ("Learn more").

### Readability

- Body text: 12pt minimum
- Legal notices: 8pt minimum
- Light text on a light background: avoid

## After the scan

The post-scan experience is 99% on smartphone. Your landing page must be ready before printing.

:::info Three non-negotiable rules
1. **Responsive** — the page adapts to small screens
2. **Fast** — loads immediately even on 3G/4G
3. **Simple** — one short form, one call to action
:::

## FAQ

::: details A retailer refuses my visual — what to do?
The retailer has veto rights over the **brand** (direct competitor, category that conflicts with their image) — not over **art direction**.

If your file meets the technical specifications — 2 mm bleed, 3 mm safe zone, 300 DPI resolution, legal notices present — it is compliant. The retailer cannot reject it for subjective reasons of taste or style.

Steps to follow:
1. Confirm your file meets the print specifications.
2. If everything is compliant, send your proof to [studio@getinside.fr](mailto:studio@getinside.fr) for official validation.

getinside validation overrides the retailer's subjective judgment.
:::

::: details How do I track campaign performance?
Your getinside dashboard gives you dual tracking:
- **Performance** — QR code scans in real time
- **Distribution** — inserted flyer volumes, updated each week by the retailer

To track sales in Google Analytics, add UTM parameters to your destination URL. Add a unique promo code per retailer for offline attribution.
:::
