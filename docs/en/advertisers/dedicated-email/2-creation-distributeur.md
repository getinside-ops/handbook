# Step 2: Production & Co-branding

Once your brief is sent, the distributor takes over to design the email.

---

## Why aren't you the one creating the email?

**The power of endorsement (recommendation).**

The goal is not to send a raw ad, but a recommendation from a trusted partner. The distributor's subscribers trust them — to maximize open and click rates, the email must follow the visual codes and usual tone of the newsletter they receive every week.

---

## What will your campaign look like?

| ✅ Expected result (Native) | ❌ What is avoided (Full Brand) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Partner Header** — Distributor's logo and menu at the top, to reassure the subscriber | 100% branded email to your site's colors, without partner mention |
| **Intro Editorial** — The distributor writes a hook sentence (*"Our favorite..."*) | Lack of contextualization by the distributor |
| **Your assets** — Visuals and offer integrated in the center, with the partner's fonts and buttons | Cognitive dissonance: the subscriber doesn't understand why they're receiving this email |

:::warning Avoid Full Brand
A Full Brand email without mentioning the partner confuses the subscriber, increases unsubscribes, and **drops performance**. Native co-branding is not a constraint — it's what makes the format effective.
:::

## Your links are dynamic

Made a mistake in the brief? Changing landing page at the last minute?

:::tip Don't panic — edit anytime
Email tracking links are **dynamic**, like getinside QR codes. You can modify the final destination URL at any time in the **"Configuration and Tracking"** tab of your campaign — even if the distributor has already embedded the link.
:::
